# Unsolved Map — Core Gameplay Loop (One-Page GDD)

**Pitch:** A GPS RPG that makes your neighborhood feel unsolved again — not by giving you things to grind, but by giving you reasons to wonder what is over there.

---

## Why Does the Player Leave Their House?

Because the real world has gone flat.

Adulthood turns the map into a solved utility diagram: gas station, grocery store, work, home. Routes become routines. Curiosity atrophies. The player leaves because the game restores a childhood question the world stopped asking:

**"I wonder what's over there?"**

Leaving is not a commute to content. It is an act of reopening the map. The game does not send the player to pins; it sends them to **edges** — the trail they have never taken, the alley behind the strip mall, the creek past the dead end, the building they have driven past a thousand times without entering. Each outing is motivated by **local mystery**: something nearby is incomplete, obscured, or wrongly assumed to be known.

The player leaves to find out whether their town still has secrets.

---

## What Reward Do They Get When They Do?

**The reward is a deepened relationship with place.**

Not a level. Not a stat sheet. The player does not power up a character — they power up their *attention*. Each outing returns something the map had hidden:

| What they bring back | What it means |
|---|---|
| **A revealed layer** | A name, story, landmark, or connection they did not know existed one block from home |
| **An expanded horizon** | Proof that knowing one thing opens three more unknowns — the more you uncover, the more you see how much remains |
| **A personal geography** | Their map stops matching everyone else's; their town becomes *theirs* |
| **A reason to return** | Not "I need 50 more of X," but "I need to see what that leads to" |

Exploration is **both the activity and the reward**. Walking is how you ask the question; arriving is how the world answers — and every answer reframes the next question.

Occasionally, a player may discover something singular: a hidden object, a one-of-a-kind find no one else has logged yet. That is the cherry on top — a memento of a place that became real to them. It is not why they left. It is proof that they were paying attention.

---

## The Core Loop

```
FLAT WORLD          →    WONDER PROMPTED     →    GO TO THE EDGE
(routine map)            ("what's over there?")     (leave the house)

        ↑                                              ↓
        
DEEPER MYSTERY      ←    PLACE DEEPENS        ←    ARRIVE & REVEAL
(more to explore)        (relationship grows)       (layer uncovered)
```

1. **The map feels incomplete** — the game surfaces nearby unknowns, not distant objectives.
2. **The player moves toward curiosity** — they walk, bike, or hike toward an edge, not a checklist.
3. **The world responds with revelation** — a story fragment, a hidden feature, a connection between two familiar places.
4. **The revelation expands the map** — what they learned makes the neighborhood *larger*, not smaller.
5. **Wonder renews** — the player now sees three new "over theres" they could not see before.

There is no finish line. The game is won when the player stops treating their surroundings as solved.

---

## How the Game Creates Wonder

- **Proximity over pilgrimage** — mystery starts within walking distance, so leaving the house is always worth it.
- **Layers, not pins** — discoveries stack; the creek leads to the ruin leads to the inscription leads to the question of who put it there.
- **The map never completes** — every revelation exposes adjacent unknowns; the UI should make *depth of mystery* visible, not *percentage complete*.
- **Personal cartography** — two players in the same town build different maps because they followed different curiosities.
- **The world was always there** — rewards feel like remembering, not looting. The game did not spawn a dragon; it showed you the door you never noticed.

---

## Design North Star

> *The player does not ask "What did I get?" They ask "What else is here?"*

That question is the product. Everything else is scaffolding to keep it alive.
